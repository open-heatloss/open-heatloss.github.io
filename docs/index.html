Open Heatloss

A computer vision dataset to fight climate change.

For the last 15 years, the French government has subsidized energy-efficient home renovations. To qualify, homeowners must obtain a detailed report from a certified thermal expert. These reports are publicly available as part of an open data initiative.

Open Heatloss is the first open academic initiative to curate, augment and distribute this data for ecological machine learning challenges.

Open Heatloss Tasks

We offer two challenges that leverage the best of the data we distribute:

  • Heat loss prediction: a single number for the raw calorific power exiting the house, normalized by house surface and average outside temperature, giving a W/K/m2. The metric is the mean squared error to the target on the test set.
  • Heat loss distribution across seven different core component: walls, windows, etc. The metric is the average cross-entropy between the predicted distribution and the target on the test set.

The ADEME DPE dataset

The ADEME manages and publishes the experts' reports on residential homes. Unfortunately, the quality of the reports is highly variable and the whole sector has been infamous in France for its unreliability. There is a lot of curation done by Ecair experts to sort out poor reports.

We will publish an initial version of this dataset by December 2024.

Satellite augmentation

The French geographical institute (IGN) publishes "satellite" (aerial) images free of charge and distribution, which we pair with the ADEME data.

The making of the dataset is dependent on the precision of the geocoding of the addresses - and it's all in all a hard task as the signal contained in the images is not always enough to do proper inference.

We will publish an initial version of this dataset by December 2024.

Google Street View augmentation

There are data challenges Google takes seriously with Street View data that hinders its academic distribution, but we're actively working with an initiative inside the organisation to make a version publicly available that doesn't break their Terms of Service.

Work in progress, expecting release by Q1 or Q2 2025

The Open Heatloss project is supported by Ecair, a tiny company operating on the French energy retrofitting market.
